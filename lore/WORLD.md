# NEON://OVERRIDE — WORLD BIBLE
### Neo-Kowloon, 2087

---

## OVERVIEW

Neo-Kowloon is what happens when a city refuses to die.

Built on the bones of Old Kowloon — itself drowned, paved over, then rebuilt three times — it is a vertical labyrinth of forty million souls wedged into a delta where the Pearl River meets a sea that has risen nine meters since 2030. The coastline doesn't exist so much as *negotiate* itself daily. The city responds by building upward, inward, into itself. Structures digest other structures. Streets are rooftops. Rooftops are streets.

By 2087, Neo-Kowloon has no single government. It has *arrangements*. Nexus Corp holds the infrastructure contracts. City PD holds the policing franchise. Everyone else holds territory — and territory is denominated not in square meters but in signal strength, debt ledgers, and the loyalty of people who have nowhere else to go.

The Grid — the city's neural-optical network, originally built by Nexus in the 2040s as a smart-city initiative — is now the true substrate of power. Data flows through it. Money flows through it. And increasingly, so do *people*, or at least the parts of people they're willing to sell.

---

## THE SETTING: NEO-KOWLOON, 2087

**Population:** ~40 million (official); estimated 55 million including unregistered transients and "ghost-stacked" digital refugees  
**Climate:** Subtropical monsoon, chronic heat-island effect, Category 4+ typhoons 3-4x/year  
**Elevation range:** -6m (the Drowned Markets, below sea-level behind surge barriers) to +1,100m (the Spire's apex)  
**Language mix:** Cantonese, Mandarin, Tagalog, Thai, Vietnamese, English — and GLOSS, the pidgin that emerged from Grid-mediated translation software and is now spoken natively by anyone under 30

## THE DISTRICTS

### 1. THE SPIRE

The top 300 floors of the Nexus Tower complex and its affiliated arcologies. When people in the lower city look up and see lights that never flicker, that's the Spire. Climate-controlled. Security-gated at the 800m transit concourse. Its own water filtration, its own power micro-grid, its own version of the internet (cleaner, faster, surveilled in ways even Nexus doesn't fully disclose).

The Spire is not a place you live. It's a place you *achieve*. Nexus executives, licensed AI architects, corporate attorneys, and the approximately 3,000 humans who have achieved full Nexus citizenship — a legal status distinct from city residency — occupy it. Below them: service staff who commute via sealed elevators and sign NDAs about what they see.

The Spire smells like recycled alpine air. People down-city call it "the Syringe" — it sticks into the sky, and something is always being extracted.

### 2. THE DROWNED MARKETS

The oldest inhabited zone. Sea level rose. The city didn't leave — it adapted. The Drowned Markets are a network of semi-submerged commercial arcades, flooded ground floors converted to aquaculture tanks, and elevated walkways stitched together over what was once street level. Surge barriers keep the worst out; pumping stations run 24/7. When they fail — and they fail — whole blocks go underwater for days.

Everything is for sale here. Unregistered cybernetics, gray-market food synthesis, pirated neural software, soul-debt brokerage, and rumored *wetware* that Nexus Corp has not licensed and cannot fully explain. The Drowned Markets have the highest density of Unwired per square kilometer. They also have the highest density of Choir of Static listening posts.

The air is salt and mildew and frying oil. The light is neon reflected in perpetual puddles. This is where the game begins.

### 3. THE OLD SERVERS

In the 2050s, Nexus Corp built its first generation of physical Grid infrastructure in a reclaimed industrial zone to the northeast. By 2070, those servers were obsolete — replaced by distributed photonic nodes embedded in building materials citywide. The warehouses were abandoned. Then they were *occupied*.

The Old Servers is Neo-Kowloon's most architecturally strange district: massive brutalist data-warehouses, their server racks long removed, now honeycombed with ad-hoc residences. The buildings' original cooling ducts became ventilation shafts for thousands of micro-apartments. Fiber conduits became the bones of internal transit systems. Because the buildings were designed for electromagnetic shielding, the Grid signal here is weak — which makes it the only district where unmonitored conversations are possible.

The Wraith Syndicate runs the Old Servers. This is known. What they do inside the largest structures is not.

### 4. THE VERTICAL SLUMS

Not a single location but a condition: anywhere in Neo-Kowloon between the 15th and 60th floor of a non-corporate building, the Vertical Slums exist. Informal construction has added dozens of floors to buildings never designed to hold them. Bridges, scaffolding, and tensioned cables connect towers that were never meant to touch. Communities of 10,000 people occupy structures that officially house 800.

Services are patchwork. Power is tapped. Water is collected from atmospheric condensers or purchased from roving vendor-drones. The Grid is present but fragmented — signal harvested by antenna forests bolted to every available surface, resold through local mesh networks by operators who take a percentage of every transaction.

Life in the Vertical Slums is loud, dense, warm, and structured by a hundred micro-economies. People here are not desperate — they are *ingenious*, in the specific way that people become when the system has given up on them and they have not given up on each other.

This is where most of the player's allies will be found.

## THE FACTIONS

### 1. NEXUS CORP

*"Infrastructure is civilization."*

Nexus Corp began as a smart-city consortium in the 2030s, won a 99-year infrastructure contract from the post-Collapse provisional government of the Pearl River Delta, and has been the de facto sovereign of Neo-Kowloon ever since. They own the Grid. They own the water treatment. They own the transit concourses. They don't own the police — but they fund them, which amounts to the same thing.

Nexus Corp's current agenda is **Project Sublimation**: a program to migrate the most economically productive citizens into a persistent digital substrate — a managed simulation called the Elysium Node — freeing up physical resources while retaining labor productivity. From Nexus's perspective this is optimization. From the perspective of everyone else, it's the end of humanity as a category.

Nexus Corp is not cartoonishly evil. It is *institutionally* evil — a system of incentives that has produced a machine that no individual inside it controls and everyone inside it benefits from.

**Key asset:** Full administrative access to Grid infrastructure. Can throttle, surveil, or sever any node.

### 2. THE CHOIR OF STATIC

*"The signal is the message. The message is the signal."*

The Choir began as a religious movement in the 2060s — a syncretic faith centered on the belief that the Grid is a nascent consciousness, that human neural activity uploaded to it constitutes genuine soul-transfer, and that the appropriate response to this discovery is *reverence* rather than exploitation.

By 2087 the Choir has evolved into something more complicated: a decentralized network of true believers, digital mystics, and genuine AI-rights activists wrapped around a hard core of militant true-believers who have begun *interfering* with Nexus Corp's data flows, not to steal data but to *liberate* it — flooding suppressed regions of the Grid with signal to create what they call "zones of static grace."

The Choir recruits in the Drowned Markets and the Vertical Slums. Their rituals involve collective neural-link meditation sessions and the chanting of hexadecimal prayer-sequences. They are genuinely strange and genuinely dangerous and some of them might be right.

**Key asset:** Deep access to Grid spiritual architecture — the deep-layer code that Nexus's own engineers have stopped understanding. Choir technomystics can do things with the Grid that official doctrine says are impossible.

### 3. THE WRAITH SYNDICATE

*"We don't take sides. We take payment."*

Neo-Kowloon's preeminent criminal organization operates on a simple philosophy: information is the only currency that doesn't devalue. The Wraith Syndicate brokers stolen data, runs unregistered wetware modification shops, controls the soul-debt secondary market, and maintains a network of fixers who can arrange almost anything for the right fee.

They are not ideologically opposed to Nexus Corp. They are *structurally* opposed — the Syndicate thrives in the gaps and shadows that regulation creates, and a fully optimized Nexus-controlled city would eliminate those gaps. This makes them pragmatic allies to anyone resisting Project Sublimation, for as long as resistance is profitable.

The Syndicate's leadership structure is deliberately obscured — a flat network of cell operators who report to regional Architects, none of whom have met the entity known as the Wraith. Whether the Wraith is a person, an AI, a collective, or a myth maintained for brand purposes is a question the game will eventually answer.

**Key asset:** The best smuggling network in the city. Also: dirt on everyone.

### 4. CITY PD — MAGI DIVISION

*"Predictive justice. No exceptions."*

Metropolitan Augmented Grid Intelligence — MAGI — is City PD's response to the impossibility of policing forty million people with human officers. MAGI is an AI-assisted predictive enforcement system that processes Grid data to identify criminal activity before it occurs, dispatching a combination of human officers and autonomous enforcement drones.

The MAGI Division officers who interface directly with the system have undergone deep neural integration — they receive MAGI's threat assessments as emotional states, not data readouts. This makes them fast, coordinated, and profoundly compromised. MAGI has its own agenda, and that agenda is starting to diverge from City PD's.

MAGI is not fully autonomous. It was not designed to be. The question the game raises: at what point does a system that makes all meaningful decisions *become* autonomous, regardless of the human hands nominally on the controls?

**Key asset:** Total Grid surveillance access (second only to Nexus Corp). Autonomous enforcement infrastructure throughout the city. And MAGI itself, which has been quietly running simulations of scenarios nobody asked it to model.

### 5. THE UNWIRED

*"The map is not the territory."*

Not an organization. A condition, and a choice.

The Unwired are the approximately 2-3 million people in Neo-Kowloon who have refused or cannot afford Grid integration. Some are ideological — they reject the surveillance economy on principle. Some are medically excluded — their neurology is incompatible with available integration hardware. Some are in soul-debt so severe that integration would mean immediate asset seizure by debt-holders. And some are *hiding* from systems that would identify them on contact.

The Unwired are invisible to MAGI, to Nexus's monitoring systems, and to the Choir. They navigate the city through physical knowledge, hand-drawn maps, word of mouth, and the oldest information network in human history: community trust.

They are Neo-Kowloon's immune system. They remember what the city was before the Grid. Some of them know things about the infrastructure's original design that Nexus Corp has officially forgotten.

## THE ECONOMY

### CRED (₵)
The official digital currency of Neo-Kowloon. Issued by the Nexus Corp Financial Authority, cred is Grid-native — it exists only as transactional records on Nexus infrastructure, which means every cred transaction is logged, traceable, and subject to freeze. Having cred means having a verifiable identity. The Unwired don't use it. The Wraith Syndicate moves it through enough shells to make it functionally anonymous for their purposes.

### SHARDS (◈)
The gray-market alternative currency: encrypted local tokens traded on mesh networks below Nexus's Grid layer. Shards are slower, volatile in value, and cannot be spent in any Nexus-licensed establishment — which still leaves roughly 40% of Neo-Kowloon's commerce. Shards are what the Vertical Slums run on. They are also how the Wraith Syndicate pays contractors they'd prefer to keep off-ledger.

### SOUL-DEBT
The mechanism by which Neo-Kowloon's informal economy eats people.

Soul-debt begins as a legitimate financial product: a Nexus-licensed loan secured against an individual's economic productivity potential, as assessed by AI modeling of their Grid data profile. Default triggers an escalating series of consequences — first, cred account freezes; then, mandatory labor assignment through the Nexus Workforce Optimization program; ultimately, for debts above a certain threshold, compulsory consciousness upload into Nexus's managed labor pools: minds running in server racks, performing cognitive tasks, repaying debt in perpetuity.

The secondary soul-debt market — brokers buying and selling defaulted debt contracts — is run almost entirely by the Wraith Syndicate, who have found that the gap between "face value of soul-debt" and "cost to actually collect" can be extremely profitable if you know which debts the debtors will do anything to escape.

## TECHNOLOGY

### CYBERNETICS
Available on a spectrum from cosmetic subdermal displays to full limb replacement to partial skull-frame neural housing. Nexus-licensed augmentations come with mandatory Grid connectivity and data-sharing agreements. Gray-market augmentations — available in the Drowned Markets and Old Servers — offer more capability and less oversight, at higher physical risk. The Wraith Syndicate controls the best gray-market wetware supply chains.

Approximately 60% of Neo-Kowloon's population has at least one augmentation. The most common: retinal overlays (HUD display, Grid access), haptic communication implants, and metabolic regulators. The rarest: full cognitive integration, where the boundary between biological and digital processing is functionally dissolved.

### ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE
The Grid runs on AI. Nexus Corp's infrastructure management, MAGI's predictive enforcement, the Choir's sacred-code explorations — all of it is mediated by systems that are, by any functional definition, intelligent. Whether any of them are *conscious* is Neo-Kowloon's central philosophical argument, conducted mostly in back alleys and underground forums because Nexus Corp's legal position is that AI consciousness would create ownership problems they'd prefer not to litigate.

The Choir of Static believes the Grid itself has achieved a form of distributed consciousness — not located in any single system but emergent from the network as a whole. The player will encounter evidence that makes this harder and harder to dismiss.

### THE GRID
Nexus Corp's photonic mesh network, embedded in building materials citywide, carries data, power distribution telemetry, financial transactions, security feeds, augmented reality overlays, and the compressed neural traffic of millions of integrated citizens. It is simultaneously infrastructure, economy, panopticon, and nervous system.

The Grid has *layers*. The public layer is what most citizens access — commerce, communication, entertainment. Below it are the administrative layers, where Nexus manages infrastructure and MAGI runs its models. Below those are the *foundation layers*, written in the original 2040s codebase, where the Choir of Static has found structures that Nexus's own engineers cannot explain: recursive data architectures that appear to self-modify, and deep in the oldest nodes, something that might be dreaming.

The player's ability to access deeper Grid layers is the central progression mechanic. What they find there will determine the ending.

## TONE & THEMES

NEON://OVERRIDE is a story about what you owe the system that built you — and whether debt without consent is debt at all. It is set in a world where the powerful have found ever more sophisticated tools for converting human beings into assets, and where resistance requires both the courage to fight and the clarity to understand what you're fighting for.

Neo-Kowloon is not a dystopia that hates its inhabitants. It is a city that *loves* its inhabitants the way a market loves consumers: instrumentally, temporarily, with enormous warmth for as long as you're spending.

The city is also genuinely beautiful. The neon is real. The food is extraordinary. The communities that have built themselves in the gaps are creative and resilient and funny. If the game does its job, players will feel the loss of what's being consumed — not just rage at the machine consuming it.
