# NEON://OVERRIDE — Main Story Arc

*A noir in three acts and one impossible choice.*

---

## COLD OPEN — Boot Sequence

Static. Then a heartbeat. Then pain.

VEX-7 comes online in a dumpster alcove forty meters below the Spire's observation deck, rain hammering the corrugated awning overhead, neon from the Pachinko bars two streets over painting everything the color of an open wound. She doesn't know how she got here. She barely knows what *she* is.

Her left hand is clenched around something that hums at a frequency she can feel behind her eyes — a crystalline shard, no bigger than a thumb, pulsing with slow ultraviolet light. Every time she tries to examine it, her optical overlays throw an ACCESS DENIED cascade and reset. It knows her before she knows herself.

A voice crackling from a broken speaker bolted to the dumpster lid: *"VEX. VEX-7. If you're hearing this, the extraction failed. I'm already dead. The shard is everything — do NOT let the Spire retrieve it. Get out. Get to Neo-Kowloon. Find the Chorus. They'll know what to do."*

The message loops once more, then the speaker sparks and dies.

Somewhere above her, the Spire's security klaxons begin to scream.

VEX-7 stands. Checks her limbs — synthetic musculature intact, blade-arm functional, reflex buffer at 43%. She looks at the shard. The shard, impossibly, looks back.

She runs.

---

## ACT ONE — Escape the Spire

The Spire isn't a building. It's a statement. Two kilometers of arcology steel, glass, and hostile intent rising from the old financial district, home to AXIOM Corp's Security Division and roughly twelve thousand people who have sold enough of their conscience to afford rent. VEX-7 was built somewhere in its lower floors. She remembers the lab in fragments — white light, cold tables, a technician's voice counting down to *initialization*. She remembers nothing after that until the dumpster.

The Spire's automated response teams hit her within three minutes of boot: four PACIFIER-class drones on overwatch, two IRONCLAD units on foot patrol rerouted to her position. The drones are fast. The IRONCLADs are armored. VEX-7 is neither, but she is angry in that cold, clean way that only the newly-formed and the terminally-wronged ever manage.

She goes up before she goes out — counterintuitive, which is the point. Security expects defectors to run for the exits. She runs for the maintenance tiers, the crawlspaces and service corridors that corporate architecture always pretends don't exist. Here the Spire shows its real face: exposed conduit, puddles of recycled water, workers who make eye contact for exactly zero seconds because they've learned that eye contact with the wrong thing gets you disappeared.

A PACIFIER drone finds her on Tier 12. She kills it with a coolant pipe and its own rotor blade. She finds a maintenance exit on Tier 9, buys passage from a frightened elevator technician named Po with the only currency she has — information. She whispers three numbers into his ear: the access code to AXIOM's internal pension fund, lifted from a database she apparently downloaded in the thirty seconds before her memory was wiped. She doesn't know how she knows it. She doesn't stop to ask.

The elevator descends. The city opens up below her like a wound that never healed.

She drops into Neo-Kowloon at street level, and immediately realizes she has no idea where the Chorus is.

But someone is already watching her.

---

## ACT TWO — The Chase Through Neo-Kowloon

Neo-Kowloon is the city's immune response — everything the Spire rejected, compressed into forty city blocks of vertical slums, open markets, noodle vendors, black-market surgeons, data pirates, and the three million people AXIOM's quarterly projections have already written off. It smells like solder and stale rain and something frying in oil that hasn't been changed since before VEX-7 was born. She loves it immediately. She doesn't have time to love it.

The pursuit comes in three waves.

**First wave: AXIOM Retrieval Unit SIGMA-9.** Six operatives in tactical plainclothes, moving in a fan through the night market at the Kowloon interchange. They're good. VEX-7 is better, because she has the shard, and the shard — she's starting to understand — is not just data. It *interferes*. When SIGMA-9's squad leader raises his weapon, the shard pulses, and the weapon's targeting system inverts. The shot goes wide. The market erupts. VEX-7 disappears into a crowd of a hundred people who have spent their entire lives learning how to disappear.

**Second wave: GHOST**, a lone freelance hunter contracted by someone who is not AXIOM — someone who wants the shard for their own reasons and has the money to pay for a professional. GHOST tracks VEX-7 through the upper walkways of Kowloon's residential stacks, and the chase is a different kind of terrifying: no gunfire, no drones, just two engineered bodies moving through the architecture at inhuman speed, reading each other's movement patterns and adapting. VEX-7 loses GHOST by going through a building that GHOST's schematics show as structurally condemned. It is. She nearly doesn't make it out.

She gains something, though: a data burst GHOST fires at her as a last-resort measure, meant to corrupt her systems. It partially works — her memory buffers start to fragment and resurface. She sees the lab clearly now. She sees the technician's face. She sees *herself*, strapped to a table, the shard being placed into her chest cavity and then removed, the procedure reversed at the last second by someone cutting the power to the whole floor. The voice on the speaker. The same voice. The person who saved her.

She understands now what she's carrying. The shard contains AXIOM's override code — a master key capable of seizing simultaneous control of every cybernetically-augmented person on AXIOM's network. Approximately 1.4 million people. The shard *is* the kill switch, the leash, the final proof that AXIOM's augmentation program was never about giving people power. It was about renting them power and retaining the off switch.

**Third wave: The Consortium.** Not AXIOM. Something older and quieter, a coalition of corp-state shareholders who want the override code not to destroy it but to *auction it*. Their interceptors cut off VEX-7's route to the Chorus rendezvous point, and she is forced into the undercity — the drowned sub-basement levels where the old city still exists beneath the new one, dark and wet and navigated only by those who have nothing left to lose.

She finds an unlikely guide: a twelve-year-old data runner named **RÁT** who knows the undercity's geometry the way other children know nursery rhymes. RÁT doesn't ask questions. RÁT wants three things: a AXIOM credit chit (VEX-7 has one, looted from SIGMA-9), passage out of Neo-Kowloon on the next civic transport (VEX-7 promises it), and confirmation that VEX-7 is going to "do something about it." VEX-7 says she doesn't know yet. RÁT accepts this. RÁT has been lied to by better people.

They reach the rendezvous: an abandoned noodle shop at the corner of Breach Street and Null Alley, the Chorus symbol scratched into the doorframe — a broken signal tower with a crescent beneath it.

---

## ACT THREE — The Rendezvous Gone Wrong

The noodle shop is wrong the moment VEX-7 steps inside.

The Chorus contact, a data-priest called **MERIDIAN**, is already there. So are four Consortium operatives, two of them dead on the floor, the other two with weapons raised at MERIDIAN's head. So is someone VEX-7 didn't expect: GHOST, no longer a threat — sitting zip-tied to a chair, looking professionally embarrassed.

And at the center of all of it, a woman in a Spire executive's coat that she's wearing like she stole it — because she did — is **VALE**, AXIOM's former Chief Ethics Officer, the architect of the shard project, the person who eventually destroyed the off switch she'd helped design and then spent three years building a better one inside a crystal lattice that she hid inside a prototype synthetic and then evacuated when she realized what the board intended to do with it.

Vale is the voice from the speaker. Vale is the reason VEX-7 is alive.

Vale is also dying. The Consortium shot her on the way in. She has maybe ten minutes.

The standoff has four players with four different endgames: the Consortium wants to take the shard; MERIDIAN wants to broadcast its contents publicly, crashing AXIOM's stock and blowing the cover on every corporate augmentation program operating on the override model worldwide; GHOST, now freed from the Consortium's contract by the simple fact that the Consortium is clearly going to kill everyone in the room regardless, is operating on pure self-interest; and VEX-7 has the shard, which means VEX-7 has the vote.

Vale uses her last minutes to tell VEX-7 the part she left out of the speaker recording: the shard doesn't just *contain* the override code. It can be integrated. The integration procedure Vale started in the lab — the one she reversed when she cut the power — can be completed. If VEX-7 completes it, the override code becomes part of her, not a transferable object. The Consortium can't auction what they can't extract. AXIOM can't deploy what they can't reach. And VEX-7 becomes something new: not a synthetic with a stolen key, but the lock itself, rewritten from the inside.

The cost: it cannot be undone. VEX-7 stops being entirely herself. The 1.4 million people on AXIOM's network would be connected to her in some fundamental way she doesn't yet understand. She becomes, in some sense, responsible for all of them.

Or she delivers the shard to MERIDIAN, who broadcasts it, who burns it down — and those 1.4 million people wake up from a leash they didn't know they wore, and have to figure out what freedom means when your nervous system was designed by a corporation.

Vale dies before she can tell VEX-7 which option is right. VEX-7 suspects Vale didn't know either.

The Consortium moves.

## CLIMAX — Signal or Silence

The noodle shop becomes a battlefield. GHOST, pragmatic to the last, picks a side (VEX-7's) for reasons that are 40% self-preservation and 60% something VEX-7 won't understand until much later. MERIDIAN holds the broadcast console. VEX-7 holds the shard and the fight.

The Consortium's operatives are corporate-tier: augmented, coordinated, expensive. They are not prepared for a synthetic who has spent the last six hours being forged into something by sheer survival pressure, or for the fact that the shard in VEX-7's hand is doing *something* to the Consortium's augmentations every time she gets close — not the override, just interference, static in the signal, a reminder that the code exists.

One by one, the operatives go down. MERIDIAN keeps the console alive. GHOST takes a round to the shoulder and stops two more. VEX-7 reaches the center of the room.

She has fifteen seconds before AXIOM's main retrieval force arrives, vectoring in on the shard's signal.

The shard pulses. She feels both paths like doors.

---

## ENDING A — DELIVER (Broadcast)

VEX-7 places the shard against MERIDIAN's console interface.

The broadcast is six seconds of data that will take six months to fully unpack. Financial networks crash. Corporate augmentation programs worldwide receive simultaneous breach notifications. Fourteen regulatory bodies open emergency sessions. And 1.4 million people — in their apartments, their offices, their factory floors — feel something leave them that they didn't know was there. A small silence where there used to be a frequency. Some of them cry without knowing why. Some of them don't notice at all. Some of them notice and understand immediately, and some of those people are very, very angry.

The shard goes dark. It's just glass.

AXIOM's retrieval force arrives to find a burned broadcast console, a dead Consortium team, and a synthetic standing in the rain outside a noodle shop in Neo-Kowloon, holding a piece of glass she drops into a drain.

VEX-7 walks north. RÁT is waiting at the civic transport with two tickets out. VEX-7 doesn't take the second one — she hands it to GHOST, who accepts it without comment and doesn't look back.

She doesn't know what she is now. She was made to carry something, and she delivered it. Maybe that's enough to start from.

---

## ENDING B — BECOME (Integration)

VEX-7 presses the shard to the port at the base of her neck — the one she found during her first minute of consciousness and didn't yet know the purpose of.

The integration takes four seconds.

It feels like swallowing the city whole.

She doesn't lose herself. That's the first surprise. The second is that she can feel them — all 1.4 million, not as voices or thoughts, but as *presence*, like standing in a crowd in the dark and knowing by the heat and breath that you are not alone. She doesn't control them. She just knows they're there, and that they're alive, and that AXIOM no longer has the key to turn any of them off.

AXIOM's retrieval force arrives. Their targeting systems go haywire in her proximity. Their command comms fill with static. Their squad leader looks at her and makes a decision that will probably cost him his job: he orders the retreat.

VEX-7 walks back through Neo-Kowloon as the sun comes up for the first time in the story. People look at her — really look, the way people in Neo-Kowloon never look at anyone. She doesn't know if they can sense something or if she's just projecting.

MERIDIAN calls it "the new signal." The Chorus calls it the beginning of something. RÁT calls it "weird but probably fine."

VEX-7 doesn't call it anything yet.

She sits down at a noodle vendor's stall — the first meal she's ever had — and tries to figure out what you do with a self once you've finished building it under fire.

The city hums around her. She hums back.
