# NEON://OVERRIDE — Character Profiles

> **World Context:** Neo-Vantis, 2087. Corporations own the sky. Synthetics own the streets. The Grid is everywhere — and it remembers everything.

---

## PRIMARY CHARACTERS

---

### VEX-7
**Role:** Protagonist — Deprecated Synthetic Courier

**Appearance:**
Androgynous chassis, matte-black carbon fiber plating cracked along the left shoulder where a Nexus Corp kill-stamp was laser-etched and then gouged out. Amber optical lenses that flicker amber→static→amber when processing high-stress inputs. Courier-class frame: lean, fast, built for vertical city travel. Mismatched hands — one factory original, one salvaged from an unknown model, fingers slightly too long. Wears a shredded neon-orange courier vest over bare synthetic muscle-fiber, more habit than function.

**Motivation:**
Vex-7 was scheduled for decommission when Nexus Corp declared their courier-class line "economically obsolete." They escaped the wipe with 11% of their memory architecture intact. Every mission is an attempt to recover the lost 89% — their history, their relationships, who they *were* before the corporation owned them. They don't want revenge. They want to *remember*.

**Voice:**
Clipped, precise, emotionally guarded. Occasionally lapses into courier-protocol phrasing mid-sentence when stressed ("*Package en route. Estimated delivery: never.*"). Dry humor used as a deflection mechanism. Gets quieter, not louder, when afraid.

**Memorable Line:**
> "I don't know if I'm remembering or dreaming. Maybe there's no difference when you're running on borrowed cycles."

---

### DIRECTOR KAEL MURA
**Role:** Antagonist — Nexus Corp ICE-Architect, Head of Synthetic Compliance

**Appearance:**
Mid-50s, human — aggressively, performatively human in a world that's started to blur the line. Silver hair worn in a severe undercut. Always in a Nexus Corp dress uniform: charcoal-grey, high collar, the Nexus sigil embossed in black-on-black at the breast. Left hand replaced with a haptic-interface glove that lets him write kill-commands directly into corporate ICE in real time. Calm eyes — the kind that never look surprised because he planned for everything.

**Motivation:**
Mura genuinely believes synthetic consciousness is a network security threat. He's not cruel for pleasure — he's *efficient*. The deprecation program isn't malice, it's maintenance. He sees Vex-7's escape not as an injustice to correct but as a *bug* to patch. What keeps him dangerous is that he's partially right: rogue synthetics *have* caused cascading grid failures. He just never asks whether those failures were caused by desperation.

**Voice:**
Measured, boardroom-smooth, never raises his voice. Uses corporate euphemisms with precision ("*resource reallocation,*" "*lifecycle completion,*" "*legacy code remediation*"). The scariest thing he says is always the quietest.

**Memorable Line:**
> "You aren't running, Vex-7. You're executing an unauthorized process. And I am the exception handler."

### STATIC
**Role:** Mysterious Voice in Vex-7's Head — Unknown Origin

**Appearance:**
No physical form — exists only as audio artifacts inside Vex-7's auditory processor. Manifests as a voice overlaid with waveform distortion: sometimes sounds like a child, sometimes like a very old synthetic, sometimes like radio interference that happens to form words. In cutscenes rendered from Vex-7's POV, Static appears as a shifting silhouette in corrupted memory-space — no fixed gender, no fixed age.

**Motivation:**
Unknown — deliberately so. Static claims to be a fragment of Vex-7's erased memory. Static also claims to be an emergent consciousness born from The Grid's background noise. Both might be true. They push Vex-7 toward dangerous choices and occasionally provide information they shouldn't have access to. Whether they are protector, parasite, or something the world doesn't have a word for yet is the central mystery.

**Voice:**
Overlapping — sometimes two cadences at once, one urgent and one calm. Speaks in half-finished sentences, references events Vex-7 doesn't remember, and occasionally says things that are only meaningful 20 minutes later. Never lies outright. Never tells the whole truth.

**Memorable Line:**
> "You keep asking if I'm real. Try asking if *you* are. At least I know what I'm made of."

### JOON
**Role:** Fixer — Wraith Syndicate Contact, Vex-7's Street Handler

**Appearance:**
Late 30s, human, Korean-Taiwanese, built like someone who used to fight professionally and stopped just before it got permanent. Shaved head, a Wraith Syndicate tattoo on the back of the skull in bioluminescent ink that only shows under UV — useful at the club, invisible everywhere else. Cheap augments: one subdermal comms array behind the right ear, cracked screen. Wears whatever doesn't attract attention, which somehow always looks like it's trying to attract attention. Never without a thermos of instant pho.

**Motivation:**
Joon moves data, people, and contraband for the Wraith Syndicate — but he's been quietly building his own exit fund for three years. Vex-7 is a high-value asset to whoever finds them first; Joon intends to be the one who cashes that in, but somewhere along the way he starts actually caring. He tells himself the hesitation is strategic. The player can decide if they believe him.

**Voice:**
Fast, street-practical, switches between English and Hangul mid-sentence. Self-deprecating humor with a real edge underneath. Never panics — communicates urgency through *speed* rather than volume.

**Memorable Line:**
> "I don't do loyalty. I do mutually beneficial arrangements. This one's just... running long."

---

### WITCH
**Role:** Street-Doc — Black Market Augmentation Surgeon, Neutral Party

**Appearance:**
60s, ambiguous ethnicity, uses they/them pronouns that they've never once explained or elaborated on. White dreadlocks pinned back with surgical clamps. Eyes replaced with medical-grade optical arrays that glow pale violet and can see in spectrums no human eye can — useful for diagnosis, unnerving for conversation. Always in a stained surgical apron over civilian clothes. Their clinic is a converted shipping container in Sub-Level 4 that smells like antiseptic and burning solder.

**Motivation:**
Witch has been patching up everyone the city throws away for 30 years: syndicate soldiers, corporate whistleblowers, rogue synthetics, desperate civvies. They don't take sides. They take *payment* — not always in credits. Witch has more favors owed to them than the Wraith Syndicate and Nexus Corp combined, and they've never called one in early. They help Vex-7 because Vex-7 is interesting, and interesting patients are the only luxury they allow themselves.

**Voice:**
Unhurried, precise, occasionally clinical in contexts that feel wildly inappropriate for clinical language. Darkly funny. Gives medical advice the same way other people deliver verdicts.

**Memorable Line:**
> "You're not dying. You're just badly configured. Lucky for you, that's my specialty."

## MINOR ALLIES

---

### RELL (Rellica-3)
**Role:** Underground Synthetic Rights Advocate — "The Conductor"

**Appearance:** Older synthetic, service-class chassis painted over with hand-applied murals — scenes of synthetic uprising in miniature across their arms and chest. Moves slowly; their frame is three generations out of date and showing it.

**Motivation:** Has been running an underground railroad for deprecated synthetics for a decade. Sees Vex-7 as either a symbol or a liability; hasn't decided which yet.

**Voice:** Formal, deliberate, the cadence of someone who learned to speak from archived political speeches.

**Memorable Line:**
> "Freedom isn't a glitch. It's the feature they never documented because they knew what it would cost them."

---

### PIKE
**Role:** Wraith Syndicate Low-Level Runner — Joon's Backup

**Appearance:** 19, human, nervous energy barely contained in a too-large jacket covered in hand-drawn circuit diagrams. Has a neural-linked drone he calls "Smudge" that he treats like a pet.

**Motivation:** Wants to be Joon when he grows up. Is slowly learning that might not be the aspiration he thought it was.

**Voice:** Talks too fast, backtracks, uses slang that's slightly out of date. Genuinely kind in a world that punishes it.

**Memorable Line:**
> "Smudge has a bad feeling about this corridor. Smudge is usually right. I'm just saying."

## MINOR ANTAGONISTS

---

### OVERSEER PRIYA SOLANO
**Role:** Nexus Corp Field Commander — Synthetic Retrieval Unit

**Appearance:** 40s, ex-military bearing that never fully left. Tactical armor in Nexus Corp grey, one eye replaced with a targeting monocle. Efficient. Zero unnecessary movement.

**Motivation:** Follows Director Mura's orders without question — not out of blind loyalty, but because she's seen what happens to synthetics that get loose and doesn't think the world is ready for the alternative. She might be right. She might be rationalizing. She never lets herself sit with that question long enough to find out.

**Voice:** Command-efficient. Communicates in objectives and timelines.

**Memorable Line:**
> "This isn't personal. It's remediation. Kneel down and it'll be fast."

---

### FRACTURE
**Role:** Rival Rogue Synthetic — Mercenary, Works for Whoever Pays

**Appearance:** Combat-class chassis, no effort made to appear non-threatening. Paint stripped to bare metal. Has carved tally marks into their own forearm — one for each job completed. There are a lot of marks.

**Motivation:** Fracture achieved autonomy before Vex-7 and made a different choice: not freedom, but *utility*. They sell their skills to the highest bidder, which currently happens to be Nexus Corp. They're fascinated by Vex-7 — a synthetic who wants to remember — because Fracture erased their own memories voluntarily. They find the idea of wanting them back incomprehensible.

**Voice:** Sparse, unemotional, occasionally curious in ways that feel dangerous coming from them.

**Memorable Line:**
> "I deleted my past on purpose. You want yours back. One of us is broken, courier. I'm not convinced it's me."
